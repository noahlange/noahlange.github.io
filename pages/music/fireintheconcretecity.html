Fire in the Concrete City

This extended play was written and recorded over the course of January and February 2014 at Luther College in Decorah, Iowa.

Sincerest thanks to Larry Lange and Julie Wrubbel-Lange, Liz and Katie Lange, Hans Becklin, Adam Sorenson, Sarah McRoberts and the staff of Chips and KWLC. Special thanks to Aaron Zauner, without whose invaluable feedback this EP would never have been finished.

All songs are copyright of Noah Lange. Anybody caught singing them without my permission will be a good friend of mine. Anyone interested in chords to these or any of my songs should email me.

1. Fire In The Concrete City

A house is afire in this concrete city
The wood is a-burnin'; the smoke billows high
You can hear all the screams from the bodies a-burnin'
Soaring up to the skies and into the night

All over this house the gasoline's hissing
A sole wooden match is blackened and charred
The paint's peeling now; sparks drop from the rafters
The fire is spreading, so fast and so far

A man standing tall, with his wife and his children
Waiting to die on their pyre alight
There's no passage out, no hope for a-livin'
No prayers but the smoke's will reach heaven now

On the door of the house a paper's a-curlin'
Small white and stabbed to the door on a nail
'This home is foreclosed,' the paper's a-screaming
But the home is afire and the family is gone

3. The Plow Broke The Plains

The dust it gathers round, a-churning in the air
The sun is blotted out, the storm's a-coming near
The crops are lying down, dead beneath the dust
The farmers have all gone; their plows begin to rust

Black rollers and the dirt scattered out across the plains
The wind's a-blowin' wild; the plow, it broke the plains
The plow, it broke the plains

And soon I will be gone driving out into the West
As blizzards billow round, I drive away through all the dust
There's nothing in the West I hear a-traveling on
No work nor food nor rest; that California's gone

Black rollers and the dirt scattered out across the plains
The wind's a-blowin' wild; the plow, it broke the plains
The plow, it broke the plains

4. I'm Doin' Fine

The wind is cold outside, babe
And you don't have to leave
And we can talk this out, babe
We can have ourselves some peace

Remember long ago babe
the sand upon the beach
the grains ran through our fingers
and my tongue it touched your teeth

Or the days of summer splendor
when the clear blue skies above
were filled with birds a-singing
all their whistling songs of love

And you don't have to do this, darlin'
And you don't have to leave
Do what you're going to do, honey
But please don't make me plead
I'll take what you can give me
And even if I cry
Don't worry about me, babe
I'm doin' fine

You used to kiss my mouth, love
In winters cold and bleak
But when you've kissed me lately
You just peck me on the cheek

And know that I would give you
All the love my heart could bear
But if love ain't worth much to you
You can give me back my share

And so goodbye my lover
And fare thee well, my friend
I wish we could be cured, love
But if not we'll have to end

And I'm sorry that I hurt you
and that's all I have to say
I'd rather not be keeping you
from other things today
But in those empty moments
If my face disturbs your mind
Don't worry about me, babe
I'm doin' fine

5. Mending

How many things do we need to be happy?
And how many dollars must each of us spend?
We're all well aware that our hearts are a-bleedin' but
I never saw a cut money could mend

How many people can we possibly punish?
And how many lives can we possibly steal?
We're all well aware that crime is a problem but
I never met a soul prison could heal

How many years must just war be a-wagin'?
And how many bombs must we drop from the sky?
We're all well aware that this world is a-hatin' but
I never fought a fight hatred could end

How many bellies must still go empty?
And how many innocent people must starve?
We're all well aware that this world is a-hungry but
I never knew a child greed could fill

How many years can we poison the waters?
How long can we keep on polluting the air?
We're all well aware that this world is a-dyin'
but rarely I meet a one giving a care
